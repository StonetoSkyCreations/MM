Location

Approach

Counselling is collaborative, grounded, and practical. Sessions focus on what matters most, what is getting in the way, and what might help bring about change.

Michael draws on solution focused counselling, cognitive behavioural therapy, and mindfulness informed practice, guided by a person centred foundation.

What this feels like

  • Clear, steady space to slow things down
  • Practical steps that fit real life
  • Respectful, client led pace

Methods are chosen for usefulness, not applied rigidly.

Frequently asked questions

What kinds of issues do you help with?

People come for many reasons. Common themes include stress, anxiety, low mood, relationship difficulties, school related challenges, life transitions, and feeling stuck or overwhelmed.

Some people arrive with a clear concern. Others know something is not working but are unsure how to name it. Both are a useful place to begin.

Is in person counselling better than online?

For many people, in person sessions feel easier and more grounding. Being in the same space can support focus and connection.

That said, online counselling can also be effective, particularly when access or schedules make in person sessions difficult. The choice depends on what feels most supportive for you.

How long does counselling usually last?

There is no fixed length.

Some people come for a small number of sessions to work through a specific issue. Others choose to attend over a longer period, especially when concerns are more complex or long standing.

The pace and duration are guided by what feels helpful and realistic.

What happens in a typical session?

Sessions are conversational and collaborative.

Time is spent understanding what is happening for you, what you have already tried, and what you would like to be different. From there, the work focuses on practical steps, new perspectives, and ways of responding that feel more manageable.

There is no requirement to talk about anything before you are ready.

Do you work with LGBTQ+ clients and diverse identities?

Yes.

Michael works with people across a wide range of identities, family structures, and relationship styles. His approach is respectful, inclusive, and responsive to individual experience.

Starting does not require having everything worked out.
