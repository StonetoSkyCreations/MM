About

Michael Multhaup is a Christchurch based counsellor and educator. He works with adults, young people, and families in private practice.

He currently serves as Head of Guidance and Student Support Leader at Hagley College, where he leads counselling services and supports student wellbeing across the school community.

The way he works

Michael offers a steady, respectful space to slow things down and make sense of what is happening.

Sessions are practical and collaborative. The focus is on understanding patterns, strengthening coping, and supporting choices that align with what matters.

The pace is not rushed. The work is shaped around the client's needs, goals, and circumstances.

Approach

Michael draws on:

  • solution focused counselling
  • cognitive behavioural therapy
  • mindfulness informed practice
  • a person centred foundation

Methods are used flexibly. The aim is not a formula. The aim is usefulness.

Experience

Michael has worked across education, community, and clinical settings, including counselling in schools and work alongside complex systems.

That experience supports a calm and measured approach, especially when life feels messy or high pressure.

Cultural context

Michael has extensive experience working in culturally diverse and bicultural environments. His practice takes account of cultural identity, family context, and lived experience.

Outside of work

Outside of counselling, Michael enjoys sailing, cycling, gardening, and practical hands on projects.
